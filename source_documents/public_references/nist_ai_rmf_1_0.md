# NIST AI Risk Management Framework 1.0

NIST describes the AI Risk Management Framework as a voluntary framework to help organizations incorporate trustworthiness considerations into the design, development, use, and evaluation of AI products, services, and systems.

## Audit-planning relevance

- AI risk management should be lifecycle-based, not limited to model launch approval.
- Trustworthy AI topics such as validity and reliability, safety, security, resilience, accountability, transparency, explainability, privacy, and fairness can be translated into R&D audit questions.
- For ACME Pharma, the framework supports a structured review of governance, intended use, data quality, monitoring, human accountability, and risk response.
- NIST is useful as a general AI-risk source, while FDA, EMA/HMA, MHRA, and GAMP/ISPE sources provide life-sciences and GxP-specific grounding.
