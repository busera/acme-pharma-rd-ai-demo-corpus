# Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 — Artificial Intelligence Act

## Authority and status

- **Authority:** European Union
- **Instrument:** Regulation (EU) 2024/1689 laying down harmonised rules on artificial intelligence
- **Official Journal date:** 12 July 2024
- **Status:** Binding EU regulation, subject to phased application and role-specific applicability
- **Official text:** <https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2024/1689/oj>
- **Official PDF:** <https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/TXT/PDF/?uri=OJ:L_202401689>

This record summarizes the enacted regulation. It does not incorporate later simplification proposals, political agreements, draft guidelines, or non-final implementation changes. Current consolidated law and application dates must be checked before use.

## Core structure

The regulation uses a risk-based framework and assigns obligations according to the AI system, its intended purpose, risk classification, and the actor's role, including provider, deployer, importer, distributor, or product manufacturer.

Key areas include:

- prohibited AI practices;
- requirements for high-risk AI systems;
- transparency duties for specified AI systems;
- obligations for general-purpose AI models;
- governance, market surveillance, enforcement, and penalties;
- AI literacy and regulatory sandbox provisions.

High-risk requirements can include risk management, data governance, technical documentation, record-keeping, deployer information, human oversight, accuracy, robustness, cybersecurity, quality management, registration, and post-market monitoring. Exact duties depend on classification and role.

## Relevance to the ACME scenario

Potential planning questions include:

- whether an R&D use case falls within the regulation's material, territorial, or role-based scope;
- whether ACME acts as provider, deployer, importer, distributor, or product manufacturer;
- whether a research, employment, biometric, safety-component, or product use triggers a listed category;
- whether model or system changes alter intended purpose or actor responsibilities;
- whether inventory, technical documentation, logs, data governance, human oversight, monitoring, and incident processes are proportionate to any applicable obligation.
