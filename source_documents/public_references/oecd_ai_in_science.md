## Authority and status

- **Date:** 26 June 2023

- **Official source:** <https://www.oecd.org/en/publications/artificial-intelligence-in-science_a8d820bd-en.html>

This record is an attributed summary with bounded excerpts. It does not reproduce the report or any complete chapter.

## Why it matters for the ACME scenario

The report considers how AI can change scientific productivity, research practices, infrastructure, skills, and governance. It is relevant to R&D planning where AI supports literature review, hypothesis development, experiment design, research assessment, or collaboration, but it does not establish a control requirement or prove how ACME operates.

## Selected source excerpts

> “Accelerating the productivity of research could be the most economically and socially valuable of all the uses of artificial intelligence (AI).”

> “The deployment of large language models (LLMs), such as ChatGPT, demands attention from policy makers as their consequences are currently uncertain.”

> “Policy should address the potential dangers entailed in dual use of AI-powered drug discovery.”

## Audit-planning considerations

- Whether AI-enabled research preserves scientific accountability rather than treating generated output as evidence.
- Whether research teams can identify source limitations, contradictory findings, negative results, and uncertainty.
- Whether access to computing, data, and AI expertise creates concentration or dependency risks.
- Whether research data are findable, accessible, interoperable, and reusable where appropriate, while confidentiality and privacy remain protected.
- Whether AI changes researcher skills, authorship, ownership, incentives, peer review, or the scientific workforce.
- Whether governance considers reproducibility, research integrity, human-AI collaboration, unequal access, and dual-use risk.

## Use boundary

Use this publication as contextual policy evidence. Do not present it as binding criteria, a pharmaceutical compliance standard, or evidence that an ACME event or control occurred. Confirm relevant legal, regulatory, contractual, and scientific requirements separately.
