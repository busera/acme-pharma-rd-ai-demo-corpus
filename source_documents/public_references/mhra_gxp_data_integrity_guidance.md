# MHRA guidance on GxP data integrity

MHRA states that its guidance covers data-integrity expectations for organizations involved in the pharmaceutical lifecycle or GLP studies regulated by MHRA. It is intended to support compliant data governance across GLP, GCP, GMP, GDP, and pharmacovigilance.

## Audit-planning relevance

- AI workflows that transform, summarize, classify, or draft from GxP-relevant records create data-integrity questions.
- Audit should assess source traceability, audit trails, retention, review, user access, original-record protection, and whether AI-generated interpretation is clearly distinguished from source evidence and human judgment.
- Data integrity is cultural and procedural as well as technical; training, pressure to accept AI output, and review quality matter.
