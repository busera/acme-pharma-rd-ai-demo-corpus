# 21 CFR Part 11 — Electronic Records; Electronic Signatures

## Authority and status

- **Issuer:** US Food and Drug Administration; published through the eCFR
- **Captured state:** Current through 9 July 2026
- **Status:** Binding regulation where applicable; the eCFR is authoritative but unofficial
- **Official source:** <https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-21/chapter-I/subchapter-A/part-11>

## Audit-planning relevance

Part 11 may matter when an AI-enabled process creates, changes, maintains, archives, retrieves, transmits, or signs electronic records required by FDA predicate rules. Planning should establish the actual record and regulatory use before applying Part 11 criteria.

Relevant evidence areas include:

- intended use and regulated-record scoping;
- system validation and ability to detect invalid or altered records;
- accurate and complete copies, retention, and retrieval;
- authorized access and segregation of duties;
- secure, computer-generated, time-stamped audit trails;
- operational checks and signature/record linkage.

## Selected source excerpts

> “(a) Validation of systems to ensure accuracy, reliability, consistent intended performance, and the ability to discern invalid or altered records.”

> “(e) Use of secure, computer-generated, time-stamped audit trails to independently record the date and time of operator entries and actions that create, modify, or delete electronic records.”

## Use boundary

Do not infer Part 11 applicability solely from pharmaceutical context or AI use. Confirm predicate-rule records, intended use, data flows, and the FDA Scope and Application guidance. Consult the current official text before reliance.
