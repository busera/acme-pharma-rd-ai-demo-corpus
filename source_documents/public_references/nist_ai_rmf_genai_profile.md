# NIST AI RMF Generative Artificial Intelligence Profile

## Authority and status

- **Issuer:** National Institute of Standards and Technology
- **Publication:** NIST AI 600-1, *Artificial Intelligence Risk Management Framework: Generative Artificial Intelligence Profile*
- **Date:** 26 July 2024
- **Status:** Voluntary, cross-sector companion to NIST AI RMF 1.0; nonbinding
- **DOI:** <https://doi.org/10.6028/NIST.AI.600-1>
- **Official PDF:** <https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/ai/NIST.AI.600-1.pdf>

## Purpose

The profile applies the AI RMF functions Govern, Map, Measure, and Manage to risks that are distinctive to or intensified by generative AI. It is intended to help organizations identify relevant risks and select proportionate actions across design, development, deployment, use, evaluation, and monitoring.

## Relevant risk areas

For the ACME R&D scenario, relevant themes include:

- confabulation and misleading generated content;
- data privacy and sensitive-information exposure;
- information security and prompt-based attacks;
- intellectual-property and provenance concerns;
- harmful, biased, or inappropriate content;
- human-AI interaction, overreliance, and automation bias;
- third-party model, dataset, and component dependencies;
- model evaluation, incident disclosure, change management, and post-deployment monitoring.

## Planning considerations

- Define context of use, accountable owners, users, affected parties, and unacceptable outcomes.
- Maintain provenance for models, data, prompts, retrieved context, evaluations, and material changes.
- Use pre-deployment testing and ongoing evaluation appropriate to the intended use and risk.
- Document limitations, uncertainty, human-review requirements, incidents, and residual risk.
- Assess supplier and value-chain dependencies rather than treating the model endpoint as the complete system.
- Reassess controls when models, retrieval corpora, data classes, users, or intended uses change.

## Use boundary

The profile is voluntary risk-management guidance. It does not create legal obligations, establish pharmaceutical GxP applicability, validate an AI system, or prove that an ACME control exists or operates effectively.
