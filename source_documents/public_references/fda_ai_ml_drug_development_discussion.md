# FDA discussion paper on AI/ML in drug and biological product development

FDA's discussion paper describes AI/ML as relevant across drug discovery, nonclinical research, clinical research, postmarket safety surveillance, and manufacturing. It emphasizes a risk-based approach, transparency, documentation across the lifecycle, and protection of public health.

## Audit-planning relevance

- AI in R&D is not limited to generic productivity; it can affect target identification, compound design, nonclinical modeling, clinical research, and manufacturing-adjacent decisions.
- Risk assessment should consider intended use, decision impact, data quality, model performance, transparency, documentation, and lifecycle monitoring.
- The paper is a discussion paper, not binding guidance; audit should use it as industry/regulatory context rather than a compliance checklist.
