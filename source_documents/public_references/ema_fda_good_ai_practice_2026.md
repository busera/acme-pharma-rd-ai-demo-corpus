# EMA/FDA Guiding Principles of Good AI Practice in Drug Development

## Authority and status

- **Issuers:** European Medicines Agency and US Food and Drug Administration
- **Date:** January 2026
- **Status:** Joint regulatory principles; non-binding
- **Official source:** <https://www.ema.europa.eu/en/documents/other/guiding-principles-good-ai-practice-drug-development_en.pdf>

## Audit-planning relevance

The ten principles provide a concise lifecycle frame for AI used to generate or analyse evidence in nonclinical, clinical, post-marketing, and manufacturing phases:

1. human-centric design;
2. risk-based approach;
3. adherence to relevant standards, including GxP where applicable;
4. clear context of use;
5. multidisciplinary expertise;
6. data governance and documentation;
7. sound model and system development;
8. risk-based performance assessment;
9. lifecycle management;
10. clear information for intended audiences.

## Use boundary

Use the principles to structure questions, not to declare compliance or universal applicability. Map each principle to the actual AI context of use, lifecycle phase, evidence role, jurisdiction, and applicable legal or GxP requirements.
