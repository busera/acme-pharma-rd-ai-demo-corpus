# FDA: Artificial Intelligence and Medical Products

## Authority and status

- **Issuer:** US Food and Drug Administration — CBER, CDER, CDRH, and OCP
- **Version:** March 2024; revised February 2025
- **Status:** Cross-center discussion paper; non-binding
- **Official source:** <https://www.fda.gov/media/177030/download>

## Audit-planning relevance

FDA describes AI management as an end-to-end lifecycle rather than a one-time model approval. The paper highlights four focus areas:

- collaboration to safeguard public health;
- regulatory approaches that support innovation;
- standards, guidelines, best practices, and lifecycle tools;
- research on AI evaluation and performance monitoring.

For the ACME scenario, this supports evidence requests for intended use, accountable lifecycle ownership, data fitness, bias and robustness evaluation, quality assurance, deployment controls, monitoring, and maintenance.

## Selected source excerpts

> “Specifically, end-to-end management of AI applications is an iterative process that starts from ideation and design and progresses through data acquisition; preparation; model development and evaluation; deployment; monitoring; and maintenance.”

> “Explore best practices for documenting and ensuring that data used to train and test AI models are fit for use.”

> “Develop a framework and strategy for quality assurance of AI-enabled tools or systems used in the medical product life cycle, emphasizing continued monitoring and mitigation of risks.”

## Use boundary

This paper informs planning but does not itself establish a binding requirement, applicability, control implementation, or operating effectiveness. Confirm the current product/use context and applicable FDA authorities.
