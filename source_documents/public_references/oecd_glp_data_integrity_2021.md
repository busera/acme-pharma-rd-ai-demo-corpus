# OECD Advisory Document on GLP Data Integrity

## Authority and status

- **Issuer:** Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development
- **Reference:** ENV/CBC/MONO(2021)26, OECD GLP Series No. 22
- **Date:** 20 September 2021
- **Status:** GLP advisory document; non-binding; scope-dependent
- **Official source:** <https://www.oecd.org/content/dam/oecd/en/publications/reports/2021/09/glp-data-integrity_c2f067ec/45779212-en.pdf>

## Audit-planning relevance

The advisory supports a lifecycle view of GLP data integrity, including:

- identification of critical data and responsible data flows;
- attributable, legible, contemporaneous, original, accurate, complete, consistent, enduring, and available data;
- metadata and electronic signatures;
- data processing, transfer, migration, review, retention, backup, and archive;
- audit-trail review, hybrid systems, access, and computerized-system roles.

## Selected source excerpts

> “The guidance aims to promote a risk-based approach to the management of data that includes data risk, criticality and life cycle.”

> “Users of this document need to understand the data flows they are responsible for or involved in (as a life cycle) in order to identify data that are likely to have impact on GLP compliance.”

> “Data integrity is the degree to which data are complete, consistent, accurate, trustworthy and that these characteristics of the data are maintained throughout the data life cycle.”

## Use boundary

This source is relevant only where activities or study phases are within GLP scope. It cannot establish that ACME data are GLP-regulated or that controls exist or operate effectively. This file is an attributed summary with bounded excerpts; OECD retains copyright in the full publication.
