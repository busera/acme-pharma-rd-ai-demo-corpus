# Training Material Outline: Responsible AI Use in R&D


## Training modules

1. Why AI in R&D is different from general productivity AI.
2. Data classes and examples of restricted research information.
3. Third-party tools, no-training terms, and vendor retention.
4. Human review and scientific accountability.
5. Intellectual property and confidential know-how risks.
6. How to record AI-assisted analysis in research records.
7. What to do when AI output looks plausible but unsupported.
8. Incident reporting and escalation.

## Scenario exercise

A researcher wants to paste unpublished experiment notes into an external AI tool to identify why a protocol failed. The exercise asks users to classify the data, identify contractual and privacy concerns, propose a safe alternative, and document the decision.

## Training gap

Current training is voluntary for pilot users. Mandatory training should be considered before expanding access.

## Training modules

1. What AI can and cannot do in ACME Pharma R&D.
2. Data classification before AI use.
3. Scientific integrity: source evidence, uncertainty, negative results, and peer review.
4. GxP and regulatory boundaries: when AI output may become a controlled-record issue.
5. Third-party and co-development rules: no-training clauses, support access, and derived artifacts.
6. Privacy and collaborator data handling.
7. How to review model output and record disposition.
8. Incident examples: data leakage, hallucinated source, inappropriate regulatory draft, and shadow AI.

## Training effectiveness checks

The training plan includes scenario questions rather than only policy acknowledgement. Users must classify examples by data class, intended use, review requirement, and escalation path. Completion alone is not treated as evidence that the control works.

## Training structure

The training material is organized into five short modules. Module 1 explains what counts as AI use in R&D, including embedded AI features in vendor platforms. Module 2 explains data classification and gives examples of public, internal, restricted, and GxP-adjacent information. Module 3 explains human accountability and how to review AI output before reuse. Module 4 explains third-party and collaboration risks, including no-training clauses, support access, and derived artifacts. Module 5 explains incident reporting and what to do if restricted data was entered into an unapproved tool.

The training uses role-based scenarios. Discovery scientists receive examples about literature summaries and experiment design. Translational teams receive examples about biomarker and coded study data. Regulatory science users receive examples about response drafting and approved-source boundaries. Project managers receive examples about governance decks and decision records.

## Training effectiveness expectations

Completion alone is not considered sufficient. The training owner plans short knowledge checks, scenario questions, and targeted refreshers for teams using higher-risk AI workflows. The material also includes "ask before use" triggers: unpublished data, partner material, patient-derived data, regulatory support, model output copied into decision records, and supplier support access.

The audit should request training completion, role coverage, knowledge-check results, exception follow-up, and whether training was delivered before system access was granted. A strong control would link completion to access provisioning for approved AI workspaces.
