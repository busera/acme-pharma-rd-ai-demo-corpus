# Research Integrity Review Minutes


## Discussion summary

The committee discussed whether AI-generated summaries may be attached to experiment records. Members agreed that AI outputs can support preparation, but the record must distinguish between source evidence, AI-generated interpretation, and scientist judgment.

## Concerns raised

- AI summaries may flatten disagreement or uncertainty in source literature.
- Negative results may be underrepresented if retrieval favors documents with clearer language.
- Researchers may accept ranked candidates without checking assumptions.
- The system may cite a document correctly while misinterpreting its significance.
- AI outputs may create discoverability or retention questions if used in patent or regulatory contexts.

## Decisions

The pilot may proceed only with mock and approved low-sensitivity material until the data classification and legal questions are closed. Any use of confidential research data requires a documented exception and business-owner approval.

## Committee discussion points

The committee accepted that AI can help scientists find sources and challenge assumptions, but it rejected the idea that AI-generated summaries can become scientific evidence by themselves. Members asked for a clear separation between original source evidence, AI interpretation, scientist judgment, and final record language.

## Decisions and open actions

| Topic | Decision | Open action |
|---|---|---|
| AI summaries in research records | Allowed only as supporting drafts, not original evidence | Define label and retention rule |
| Candidate ranking | Allowed as advisory input | Require rationale and scientist override log |
| Negative results | Must be explicitly searched and documented | Build test cases using failed experiments and inconclusive studies |
| Protocol suggestions | Must be reviewed by protocol owner | Add sign-off field to output review template |
| Regulatory wording | Not approved for submission-ready use | Regulatory Science to define controlled pilot boundary |

## Meeting discussion detail

The Research Integrity Committee discussed whether AI-generated summaries could unintentionally become scientific evidence when copied into governance decks or project notes. Committee members agreed that AI output may be useful for challenging assumptions, finding contradictory publications, and improving consistency of study planning, but it must not replace primary evidence review. The committee asked for clearer labeling of AI-assisted sections in internal decision packets and a requirement that accountable scientists confirm source evidence before relying on the output.

A second discussion focused on uncertainty. Researchers reported that draft AI outputs often sound more complete than the evidence supports. The committee requested that outputs show source limitations, contradictory findings, and open questions rather than only a polished synthesis. This is especially important for early research where negative evidence, failed experiments, and ambiguous assay results may be more informative than positive findings.

## Actions assigned

The committee assigned actions to R&D Digital, Quality, and Scientific Governance. R&D Digital will update user guidance to require source review before reuse. Quality will define when AI output becomes part of a controlled record. Scientific Governance will develop review expectations for outputs used in candidate review, experiment design, or regulatory response preparation.

The audit should request action closure evidence, revised guidance, meeting follow-up, and examples of AI-assisted decision material. The key question is whether integrity expectations have moved from committee discussion into everyday operating practice.
