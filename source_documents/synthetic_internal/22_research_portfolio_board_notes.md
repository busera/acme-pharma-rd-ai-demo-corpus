# Research Portfolio Board Notes on AI Pilot

**Record owner:** Research Portfolio Board Secretariat
**Approver:** Research Portfolio Board Chair
**Status:** Approved meeting notes
**Effective or as-of date:** 24 June 2026
**Review cycle:** Not applicable

## Decisions requested

- Define the pilot success criteria before expanding beyond two research groups.
- Keep candidate prioritization out of scope until validation is stronger.
- Require Legal confirmation before any vendor receives confidential research data.
- Ask Security to review the cloud architecture before pilot launch.
- Ask R&D Quality to define documentation expectations when outputs influence planning.

## Board discussion

The board sees value in AI-supported literature review and experiment challenge, but members raised concerns about false confidence, uneven adoption across therapy areas, supplier dependency, and whether use-case owners are underestimating GxP and IP boundaries. The board asked for a readiness checkpoint before any team uses AI output in portfolio-ranking materials.

## Readiness conditions before expansion

1. Use-case register complete for the top five R&D functions.
2. Contract and no-training language approved.
3. Data-flow diagram independently reviewed.
4. GxP trigger decision path agreed with Quality and Regulatory.
5. Negative-evidence retrieval test cases passed.
6. Shadow AI survey action plan approved.
7. Exit and deletion evidence tested with supplier.

## Board discussion detail

The Research Portfolio Board reviewed whether AI-enabled candidate prioritization could be used in early portfolio discussions. Board members supported using AI to surface evidence gaps, compare competing hypotheses, and identify contradictory findings. They rejected using AI-generated ranking as an automatic gate. The board requested that any ranking include source traceability, uncertainty flags, and clear separation between model-generated suggestions and accountable scientist judgment.

The board also discussed Project NEURALIS. The main concern was not the user interface but whether co-developed evaluation sets, reviewer feedback, and prompt patterns could reveal ACME's research strategy. Legal/IP asked for a retained-artifact register before broader testing. Quality asked for a GxP boundary assessment before outputs are reused in regulated development records.

## Decisions and follow-up

The board approved continued sandbox testing for two use cases and asked R&D Digital to return with inventory reconciliation, supplier evidence, and monitoring metrics. It did not approve expansion to routine use. The board requested a view of live systems versus systems in development because current status reporting mixed use cases, platforms, and experiments.
